Competition Overview

Everything you need to know about the AMC 8

üìã

Format

The AMC 8 consists of 25 multiple-choice questions with five answer choices each. Students have 40 minutes to complete the exam, which tests problem-solving skills and mathematical knowledge.

üéØ

Eligibility

Open to students in grade 8 and below. There is no minimum grade requirement, making it accessible to talented younger students who are ready for the challenge.

üìä

Scoring

Each correct answer earns 1 point. There is no penalty for incorrect answers or unanswered questions. The maximum possible score is 25 points.

üèÜ

Recognition

Top scorers receive awards including Achievement Rolls, Honor Rolls, and Distinguished Honor Rolls. High-performing students may qualify for invitations to math camps and programs.

üìÖ

When

The AMC 8 is typically held in November each year. Registration opens in the late summer, and schools or organizations can administer the exam.

üåü

Why Take It

The AMC 8 builds problem-solving skills, mathematical confidence, and provides a foundation for future competitions like the AMC 10 and AMC 12.
